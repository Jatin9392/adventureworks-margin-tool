The business question

What happens to profit when we offer a discount?

Worked example

Move the discount and follow the money

All-Purpose Bike Stand

List price$159.00
Standard cost$59.47
0%60%
New selling price$143.10
Gross profit$83.63
New margin58.4%
Margin movement-4.2 points
Cost$59.47
Profit$83.63

1. New selling price

List price minus the discount amount.

$159.00 - 10% = $143.10

2. Gross profit

New selling price minus product cost.

$143.10 - $59.47 = $83.63

3. Margin

Gross profit divided by the new selling price.

$83.63 / $143.10 = 58.4%
Margin is not markup. Margin divides profit by selling price. Markup divides profit by cost. This tool shows both, but the discount decision focuses on margin.

Future business version

Live SQL connection

A secure middle service reads the latest approved values from SQL Server whenever the page requests them.

Why a middle service? A public webpage should never receive the SQL Server password or direct database access. The technical term for the secure middle service is an API.

Your presentation

A 60-second explanation for your manager

“This tool helps us understand the financial impact of a proposed discount before we offer it. We choose a product, adjust the discount, and immediately see the new selling price, gross profit, and margin. The current shared version uses a saved sample of AdventureWorks data. A production version could securely connect to our SQL database so approved product costs and prices stay current.”
